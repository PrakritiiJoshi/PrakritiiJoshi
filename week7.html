Reading Responses

Key Concepts of Museology In this reading the author discusses key concepts in museology in alphabetical order. First the author defines the concepts and then gives an explanation. The author first discusses architecture- which is defines as the art of designing and installing or building a space that will be used those specific museum functions. The form and function of a museum is important. The second concept is collection which is defined as a a set of material or intangible objects which an individual or an establishment has assembled, classified, selected, and preserved in a safe setting. Third, the author discusses communication as a key concept which I think is the most interesting and defines it as the action of conveying information between one or several emitters and one or several receivers through a channel. The author goes on to discuss the chronic lack of interactivity in museum communication which leads to questions on how people can make the visitor more active while seeking participation. I think one crucial way to do this is by implementing technology so the visitors can have more options and control more of what they want to see.
