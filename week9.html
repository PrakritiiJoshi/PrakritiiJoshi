Reading Response

From Malraux's Imaginary Museum to the Virtual Museum - This reading talks about how a virtual museum allows us to carry out a series of instructive actions that exceed the limits of photographic reporduction. The reader differentiated a real visit versus a virtual one. Photogrpahic or digital reproductions are not original works but more or less successful replicas on different support systems.

Behind the scenes of the museum's website - This reading examines the role of online resources hosted by museums as a means to communicate and reach out to new audiences. This article examines museum websites from a new perspective; drawing upon the field of ‘critical code studies’, to analyze the online exhibition “Ancient Cyprus in the British Museum”, to illustrate this value of this approach.This reading discusses the style of the HTML coding as one language which provides a sense of power and possession over the contents of the website- corresponding to a consumerist model of the museum experience. The online resource shapes the visitor experience in a “analogous” manner to a traditional museum display as visitors are provided with a structure to posses and own the objects on display. The author explains how the examination of digital objects and the wider issues of digital heritage muse ground itself in the technology used to view and access information.
